# Kraken Chat — License FAQ

Q: Can I use Kraken Chat for free for personal or home use?  
A: YES! Self-hosters and individuals may use Kraken Chat freely under the AGPLv3 license.

Q: I'm a student, educator, or non-profit. Do I need a commercial license?  
A: NO. Use under AGPLv3 is free for academic and non-profit organizations.

Q: I want to run Kraken Chat in my startup, business, or SaaS offering. Do I need a license?  
A: YES. Any use by a for-profit business, company (of any size), or managed hosting/SaaS requires a commercial license.

Q: I want to modify or contribute to Kraken Chat. Do I still need a commercial license?  
A: Contributions and modifications are always welcome under the AGPLv3, but company/private use (commercial) follows the rules above.

Q: I want to host Kraken Chat for myself, my friends, or my community. Do I need a commercial license?  
A: NO. Personal, hobby, and self-hosted instances—including those you run for free for your own community or friends—do NOT require a commercial license and are always permitted under AGPLv3.

Q: I want to host Kraken Chat as a free public service—do I need a commercial license?  
A: NO. Hosting Kraken Chat as a free, open, non-profit community service is allowed under the AGPLv3.

Q: I want to offer Kraken Chat as a paid (monetized or for-profit) hosted service to others. What are my obligations?  
A: You must either make your full service source code available (AGPLv3 compliance), OR purchase a commercial license.

Q: I want to charge money for access, run Kraken Chat as a business/SaaS, or offer it as part of a commercial product.  
A: In this case, you MUST purchase a commercial license.

Q: I'm still unsure?  
A: Email licensing {at} krakenchat [dot] app for clarification!
